# 04 - What Is EMA?

## Goal

Understand what an EMA really does.

---

EMA stands for:

**Exponential Moving Average**

Don't worry about the long name.

The important idea is much simpler.

---

## Imagine You Are Flying in an Airplane

You look down at the ocean.

The ocean has thousands of tiny waves.

```text
~~~~ ~~~~ ~~~~~ ~~~~
```

From the airplane,

you don't focus on every tiny wave.

You look at the **overall direction** of the ocean.

---

Now imagine standing on the beach.

Every tiny wave looks important.

It feels confusing.

---

The market is the same.

Price moves up...

Down...

Up...

Down...

every minute.

EMA helps remove that small "noise" so you can see the overall direction more clearly.

---


---

## What Does EMA Tell Us?

If EMA is moving upward,

buyers have generally been stronger recently.

If EMA is moving downward,

sellers have generally been stronger recently.

EMA does **not** predict the future.

It only summarizes what price has been doing.

---

## Important

EMA is **not** a Buy signal.

EMA is **not** a Sell signal.

EMA simply helps us understand the current trend.

---


# What Does the Number Mean?

This is the most important part.

When you see:

* EMA 20
* EMA 50
* EMA 200

The number tells the EMA **how many candles** to use.

It is **not** 20 minutes or 20 dollars.

It means **20 candles**.

---

## Example 1 — 1 Minute Chart

If you are looking at a **1-minute chart**:

* EMA 20 = last **20 one-minute candles**
* EMA 50 = last **50 one-minute candles**

---

## Example 2 — 1 Hour Chart

If you are looking at a **1-hour chart**:

* EMA 20 = last **20 one-hour candles**
* EMA 50 = last **50 one-hour candles**

---

## Example 3 — Daily Chart

If you are looking at a **daily chart**:

* EMA 20 = last **20 daily candles**
* EMA 50 = last **50 daily candles**

The timeframe changes, but the meaning stays the same.

The number always represents the number of candles.

---

# Why Do Different EMAs Move Differently?

Imagine two people watching a football team.

Person A only watches the **last 5 matches**.

Person B watches the **last 50 matches**.

Who reacts faster if the team suddenly starts losing?

**Person A.**

Why?

Because they are only looking at recent matches.

The EMA works the same way.

---

## EMA 20

EMA 20 only pays attention to the last 20 candles.

It reacts quickly.

If price suddenly changes direction, EMA20 changes direction sooner.

---

## EMA 50

EMA 50 looks at the last 50 candles.

It changes more slowly.

It ignores many small movements.

---

## EMA 200

EMA 200 looks at the last 200 candles.

It changes very slowly.

It shows the long-term trend.

---

# Imagine Three Cars

Three cars are driving on the same road.

Car 1 turns very quickly.

This is like **EMA20**.

Car 2 turns more slowly.

This is like **EMA50**.

Car 3 is a huge truck.

It takes a long time to turn.

This is like **EMA200**.

The bigger the number, the slower the EMA reacts.

---

# Which One Is Better?

There is no "best" EMA.

Each one has a different job.

| EMA     | Purpose                 |
| ------- | ----------------------- |
| EMA 20  | Shows short-term trend  |
| EMA 50  | Shows medium-term trend |
| EMA 200 | Shows long-term trend   |

---

# Why Do Traders Use Two EMAs?

Imagine you ask two people about the weather.

One tells you what happened today.

The other tells you what has been happening all month.

Using both gives a better picture.

Trading is similar.

Many traders compare:

* EMA20 (fast)
* EMA50 (slow)

If EMA20 is above EMA50, the recent price is stronger than the medium-term price, suggesting buyers have been stronger recently.

If EMA20 is below EMA50, recent price is weaker than the medium-term price, suggesting sellers have been stronger recently.

---

# Important

EMA does **not** predict the future.

It only summarizes what price has already done.

It is a tool for understanding the trend, not a guarantee that the trend will continue.
