# 1. How Markets Move

Imagine standing on a beach.

The ocean is always moving.

Sometimes huge waves travel toward the shore.

Sometimes there are only tiny waves.

The stock market behaves in a similar way.

The **main movement** is called the **Trend**.

The **small movements against the trend** are called **Pullbacks**.

Example:

```
        ↑
      ↑
    ↑
  ↑
↑
```

The market is moving upward.

Now imagine a small wave.

```
        ↑
      ↑
    ↓
  ↑
↑
```

That small downward movement is not a reversal.

It is simply a pullback.

Professional traders wait for these small pullbacks before joining the larger trend.

---

# 2. What Is a Trend?

A trend is the overall direction of the market.

There are only three market conditions.

## Uptrend

The market creates:

- Higher Highs
- Higher Lows

Example

```
        HH
       /
HL   /
    /
HH /
  /
HL
```

Buyers are stronger than sellers.

---

## Downtrend

The market creates:

- Lower Highs
- Lower Lows

Example

```
LL
 \
  \
   LH
     \
      LL
```

Sellers control the market.

---

## Sideways Market

Price moves inside a range.

```
────────────
\/\/\/\/\/\/
────────────
```

Neither buyers nor sellers are winning.

EMA Trend Pullback should usually avoid these markets.

---

# 3. What Is a Pullback?

A pullback is a temporary move against the main trend.

Imagine climbing a mountain.

You don't walk uphill in one straight line.

Sometimes you stop.

Sometimes you step backward.

Then you continue climbing.

Markets behave exactly the same.

Example

```
      ↑
    ↑
  ↓
↑
```

The market pulls back briefly before continuing upward.

A pullback is healthy.

It allows new buyers to enter.

---

# 4. What Is EMA?

EMA stands for

**Exponential Moving Average**

An EMA follows price like a flexible rope.

Imagine walking your dog.

You suddenly turn.

Your dog doesn't instantly change direction.

It follows you with a slight delay.

Price is you.

EMA is the dog.

Because EMA reacts quickly, traders use it to understand trend direction.

---

# 5. Why Do Traders Use Different EMAs?

Each EMA tells a different story.

## EMA9

Very fast.

Shows immediate momentum.

Good for aggressive traders.

---

## EMA20

Short-term trend.

Often acts as support during strong trends.

---

## EMA50

Medium-term trend.

Shows whether the market is still healthy.

---

## EMA100

Long-term trend.

Confirms stronger market direction.

---

## EMA200

The most popular trend filter.

Think of EMA200 as the ocean current.

Small waves may move in different directions.

The ocean current still flows the same way.

Professional traders rarely trade against the EMA200.

---

Imagine five cyclists riding together.


Everyone is moving in the same direction.

That is a healthy trend.

Bullish alignment

```

>

EMA50

>

EMA100

>

EMA200
```

Bearish alignment

```
EMA20

<

EMA50

<

EMA100

<

EMA200
```

The better the alignment, the stronger the trend.

---

# 7. What Is an EMA Trend Pullback?

Imagine surfing.

A surfer doesn't fight the ocean.

They wait for the right wave.

Then they ride it.

Trading is similar.

We don't chase price.

We wait for price to return toward the EMA.

When buyers step back in, we join them.

This is called an EMA Trend Pullback.

# 9. Good Example

```
Trend ↑

Price ↑

↓

Pullback

↓

Buy

```

Everything follows the main trend.

# 10. Bad Example

```
Price above EMA200

↓

Huge pullback

↓

EMA alignment broken

↓

Buy anyway
```

This is dangerous.

The trend may already be ending.

---

# 11. Common Mistakes

❌ Buying after a huge green candle.

❌ Trading against EMA200.

❌ Ignoring EMA alignment.

❌ Trading inside sideways markets.

❌ Chasing price instead of waiting for pullbacks.

---

# 12. Trading Checklist

Before every trade ask:

✅ Is price above EMA200?

✅ Are EMAs aligned?

✅ Is the market trending?

✅ Has price pulled back?

✅ Is there a bullish confirmation candle?

✅ Is Risk Reward at least 2:1?

If every answer is YES, the setup matches the EMA Trend Pullback Strategy.

# Summary

The EMA Trend Pullback Strategy is based on one simple idea:

> **Trade with the trend, not against it.**

Professional traders don't chase the market.

They wait patiently for a pullback, confirm that the trend is still healthy, and then enter with the direction of the larger move.

Patience is often the difference between a beginner and a professional trader.
